Top Attractions

Masaya National Park

Masaya National Park is home to the impressive Masaya Volcano, one of Nicaragua's most active and accessible volcanoes. The park offers a unique opportunity to drive right up to the edge of a volcanic crater and witness the smoking Santiago crater. In addition to the volcano, the park features a museum, hiking trails, and the chance to spot local wildlife. At night, visitors can observe the mesmerizing glow of the lava lake in the volcano's crater.

Granada

A colonial gem, boasting colorful architecture and a rich history. As one of the oldest cities in the Americas, it offers a charming blend of Spanish colonial landmarks, cobblestone streets, and a bustling city center. Visitors can explore historical sites like the grand Cathedral of Granada, enjoy a boat tour around the islets of Lake Nicaragua, and experience the local culture and cuisine in the city's many cafes and markets.

San Juan del Sur Beach

A picturesque coastal town known for its stunning crescent-shaped bay and vibrant beach life. Renowned for its excellent surfing conditions, the beach attracts enthusiasts from around the world. The laid-back atmosphere, combined with a variety of beachfront restaurants and bars, makes it a perfect destination for travelers looking to relax and enjoy the Pacific coastline.
